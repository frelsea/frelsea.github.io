Dry bulk asset values continued to appreciate across all size classes in week 17, with Capesize and Ultramax resale prices posting double-digit year-on-year gains, while tanker secondhand markets saw high transaction volumes including several large en-bloc deals across Aframax and MR2 segments.

## Dry Bulk

Five-year-old Capesize vessels are assessed at $70.5 million, up 12% year-on-year, while resale units command approximately $81 million, a 7% increase from the same period in 2025. Ten-year-old Capesize tonnage has appreciated more steeply, reaching $54.5 million, a 22% gain on 2025 levels. The appreciation in Capesize values continues to outpace equivalent freight earnings, pointing to sustained owner confidence in long-term supply-demand fundamentals rather than near-term rate expectations. Two 2012-built VLOCs of 205,000 dwt each changed hands en bloc for in excess of $90 million to Chinese interests.

Kamsarmax and Ultramax resale values are each assessed at approximately $42 to $42.5 million, up around 11% year-on-year. Transaction activity was concentrated in the Ultramax and Supramax segments. A modern 64,000-dwt 2018-built Ultramax sold for $32 million, and two 2027-built resale units achieved $36.5 million each to Far Eastern buyers. Several 2011 to 2012-built Japanese-built Ultramaxes traded between $18.5 million and $20.4 million, while a 60,000-dwt 2016-built unit achieved $26 million. In the Kamsarmax sector, an 88,000-dwt 2006-built vessel sold for $12.9 million to Chinese buyers, a 76,000-dwt 2012-built unit achieved $16.5 million, and an 87,000-dwt 2010-built geared unit was reported at mid-$16 million. Older 2006 to 2007-built Supramax tonnage changed hands between $9.8 million and $14 million depending on condition and class position. In the Handysize segment, 2014 to 2015-built units achieved $17 to $18 million.

## Tankers

Tanker secondhand values have risen more sharply than dry bulk equivalents in percentage terms. Suezmax resales are assessed at $175 million, up 21% year-on-year, and five-year Suezmax units at $120 million, reflecting the same annual gain. Aframax resales stand at $88.8 million, up 20% year-on-year, and MR2 resales at $53.5 million, up 7%.

Transaction volumes in the tanker sector were high. Two 2026-built Suezmax resale vessels achieved $120 million each to Greek buyers, and a pair of 2014-built Suezmax units changed hands en bloc for $140 million. An older 2007-built Suezmax was reported at $50.1 million. Three 2014-built Aframax units transacted en bloc at $195 million, and a 2018-built Aframax achieved $78 million. A 2006-built VLCC of 302,000 dwt sold for $60 million. In the LR1 segment, a pair of 2007-built units sold at $20 million each, a 2008-built unit achieved $19 million to Greek buyers, and a 2006-built unit was reported at $16.5 million. MR2 activity was particularly broad: a 2026-built resale unit achieved approximately $50 million, three 2014-built Korean-built MR2 units transacted en bloc at $105 million to Turkish interests, a 2011-built unit sold at $25.5 million and a 2010-built unit at $20.8 million. Older 2006-built MR2 tonnage traded at $13.3 million, and a separate 2010-built unit achieved $22 million to Greek buyers.

## Demolition and Newbuilding

Demolition values were stable across all destinations during the week. Indian subcontinent rates for bulkers ranged from $440 to $480 per light displacement tonne, with tankers assessed at $420 to $480 per light displacement tonne. Bangladesh offered $360 to $480 for bulkers and $390 to $510 for tankers. Pakistan assessed bulkers at $270 to $400 and tankers at $280 to $440 per light displacement tonne. Turkey remained the lowest-returning destination, with bulker rates between $270 and $460 per light displacement tonne. Enquiry in the recycling market was limited. A 1996-built bulk carrier changed hands at approximately $439 per light displacement tonne to a Bangladeshi buyer, and a 1998-built Panamax-sized bulker was reported sold as-is in Indonesia at $461 per light displacement tonne.

Newbuilding prices were broadly flat week-on-week. Capesize orders at Chinese yards are quoted at $75 to $77 million, Kamsarmax at $37 to $38 million, Ultramax at $34 to $35 million and Handysize at $30 to $33 million. Korean-built VLCCs are priced at $131 million, Suezmax at $89 million, Aframax at $75.5 million and MR2 at approximately $51 million. Contracting activity was active during the week. Four plus two Capesize units were ordered at an Imabari yard at $39.2 million each for 2028 to 2029 delivery. Two LR2 tankers were placed at a Chinese yard at $76.5 million each for 2028 delivery, and two MR2 tankers at a Korean yard at approximately $51 million each. Eight VLCC-equivalent units were contracted at a Chinese yard at $123 million each for 2028 to 2030 delivery. A South Korean shipbuilder received an order for four stainless-steel chemical tankers from a Norwegian operator at approximately $72.5 million each for 2027 delivery.
