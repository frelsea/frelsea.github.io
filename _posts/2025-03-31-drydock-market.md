title: "What the drydock market tells you about OPEX discipline"
date: 2025-03-31
---

Shipyard capacity in Southeast Asia is running tight through Q3 2025. For owners planning a drydock this year, slot availability is limited and steel prices remain elevated. The result is that undisciplined OPEX managers — those who deferred maintenance to protect short-term earnings — are now facing compressed windows, inflated quotes, and classification societies pushing back on condition surveys. The gap between a well-run vessel and a poorly-run one is now very visible at drydock.

The pattern is consistent. When rates are strong, deferred maintenance hides itself. P&L looks clean, the vessel keeps trading, and the technical manager's monthly reports stay green. The reckoning comes at drydock. A vessel arriving with accumulated deferred items — structural repairs, coating failures, overdue pipe renewals — will spend 15 to 25 days longer in dock than planned. At current yard rates in Singapore and Batam, that is an additional $300k–$500k in unbudgeted costs, before any scope additions from the class surveyor.

> Drydock is the audit that cannot be postponed.

For owners buying secondhand tonnage, drydock history is one of the most diagnostic data points available. Not the headline cost — the scope breakdown. A vessel whose previous owner consistently hit budget and schedule is a different animal from one that has a pattern of overruns. The latter is a signal about how the vessel has been managed across every other dimension: lubricant consumption, spare parts inventory, crew competence, maintenance planning.

This matters most in the current market because drydock costs are now large enough to affect acquisition economics. A buyer who does not stress-test the drydock budget in their investment model — using actual yard quotes, not historical averages — will find the first special survey eats a material portion of their expected return.

The advice is simple: before you buy, get the records, send an inspector, and get a realistic drydock estimate from a yard that has actually quoted on comparable vessels recently. The number will surprise you. That is the point.
