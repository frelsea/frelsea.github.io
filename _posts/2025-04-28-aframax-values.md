title: "Aframax values are holding — but the buyers have changed"
date: 2025-04-28
---

Secondhand Aframax prices in the 2008–2012 age bracket have held remarkably firm through Q1 2025, even as TD7 and TD17 rates softened from their 2024 peaks. The transactions are still closing. The shift is in who is buying. Greek and Turkish independents that drove the 2022–2023 run have largely stepped back. What is filling the gap is a mix of trading house-linked tonnage and first-time buyers from the Gulf — buyers who are less rate-sensitive and more focused on long-term positioning.

The data bears this out. A 2010-built Aframax changed hands in March at $38.5M — a number that would have looked aggressive eighteen months ago, but cleared without drama. The sellers were a mid-size Greek operator running four vessels. The buyer, through a Singapore-registered SPV, is widely understood to be trading house-backed. That structure — the SPV, the discretion, the absence of broker fanfare — is increasingly the signature of this buyer class.

> When the rate environment softens and the price environment does not, the question is always: who is holding the conviction, and why?

For owners on the sell side, the implication is clear. The pool of motivated buyers has not evaporated — it has shifted. The trading houses and Gulf entrants are buying on fundamentals: fleet age profile, replacement cost, the structural supply constraint baked in by the order book. They are not chasing rates. They will not be pressured by a soft quarter. That changes the dynamics of a sale process. You cannot market these assets the same way you would to a Greek buyer running twelve vessels — you need a different conversation, a different brief, and often a different network.

The Aframax order book remains thin. Deliveries through 2026 will not meaningfully change the fleet age profile. On current demolition trends, the sub-20-year fleet will tighten further by late 2026. Buyers who understand this are acquiring now. The asset play is still live — it has simply moved to a different desk.
